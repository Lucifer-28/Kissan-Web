PRADHAN MANTRI FASAL BIMA YOJANA

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is a crop insurance scheme launched by the Government of India to provide insurance coverage and financial support to farmers in case of crop loss due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases.

PMFBY aims to provide a comprehensive insurance cover against failure of the crop thus helping in stabilising the income of the farmers. The Scheme covers all Food & Oilseeds crops and Annual Commercial/Horticultural Crops for which past yield data is available and for which requisite number of Crop Cutting Experiments (CCEs) are being conducted under General Crop Estimation Survey (GCES). The scheme is implemented by empanelled general insurance companies. Selection of Implementing Agency (IA) is done by the concerned State Government through bidding. The scheme was earlier compulsory for loanee farmers availing Crop Loan /KCC account for notified crops and voluntary for other others, but has been made voluntary since 2020 when reforms in the scheme were introduced.[2] The scheme is being administered by Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare. The scheme has been beset by a number of problems for the farmers with unpaid dues in thousands of crores while insurance companies have pocketed the money.

Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Sanchar Kaushalya Yojana (PMKVY)

The Pandit Deendayal Upadhyay Sanchar Kaushalya Yojana (PMKVY) is a skill development scheme launched by the Government of India to provide training and certification to farmers and rural youth in various agricultural skills such as dairy farming, poultry farming, horticulture, and more.

Full social inclusion of the candidates is ensured by the mandatory coverage of socially disadvantaged group. 50% of the funds would be earmarked for SCs and STs, 15% to minorities and 3% for persons with disabilities. One third of the persons covered should be women. Regional inclusion of candidates is enabled through a special sub scheme for the youth of Jammu and Kashmir called Himayat, which MoRD operates under ASDP for state which covers urban as well as rural youth and Below Poverty line (BPL) as well as Above Poverty line (APL) persons. Moreover, Roshni - a special scheme for tribal areas and critical Left Wing Extremist (LWE) affected districts with separate guidelines has been launched that takes into account the peculiar situation in selected critical LWE districts. Particularly it provides training for different time periods.

Maharashtra State Agriculture Marketing Board (MSAMB)

The Maharashtra State Agriculture Marketing Board (MSAMB) is a government agency responsible for promoting and developing agricultural marketing in Maharashtra. It provides various services and facilities to farmers such as market information, grading, packaging, and more.

1. To advise the market committees for making improvements in their functioning and make available financial assistance for the same, so as to develop better agriculture marketing system.
2. To make available the necessary technology to various Fruits & Vegetable Marketing Co-operatives and also help them to set up their projects such as Precooling and Cold Storage, Value Addition Centers, etc.
3. Import various types of Seeds, Samplings, Bulbs, and Agro-chemicals etc. as per the demand of farmers and to make them available at reasonable rates.
4. Computerization of action process & office activities of APMCs.
5. Software development and implementation for various activities of State Marketing Board, Directorate of Marketing & APMCs.
